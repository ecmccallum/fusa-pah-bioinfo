Interpretation

QUAST verdict — excellent contiguity. The N50 of 635 kb is outstanding for an Illumina-only assembly. Only 24 contigs are needed to span half the genome. Total assembly size (57.4 Mb) and GC content (53.6%) are both consistent with published Fusarium solani genomes.
BUSCO verdict — near-complete gene space. 99.4% completeness against the Hypocreales lineage database is excellent. The low duplication rate (0.7%) indicates no significant contamination signal. Only 23 of 4,494 expected genes are missing entirely.
Flag — internal stop codons (6.9%). 309 complete BUSCOs contain internal stop codons. This is likely a consequence of residual Methylorubrum populi reads (identified in Stage 2, ~36% of raw reads) introducing frameshifts in a small proportion of gene models. This does not affect overall assembly quality assessment but should be noted in the write-up.

Commands run

quast.py ~/fusarium_pah/results/assembly/scaffolds.fasta \
  --eukaryote \
  --fungus \
  --threads 8 \
  -o ~/fusarium_pah/results/quast

busco \
  -i ~/fusarium_pah/results/assembly/scaffolds.fasta \
  -o fusarium_busco \
  --out_path ~/fusarium_pah/results/ \
  -l hypocreales_odb10 \
  -m genome \
  --cpu 8

Decision

Assembly quality is sufficient to proceed. Both contiguity (QUAST N50 = 635 kb) and gene completeness (BUSCO 99.4%) meet the standards expected for a high-quality fungal genome assembly. Proceeding to Stage 5 — Funannotate functional annotation.

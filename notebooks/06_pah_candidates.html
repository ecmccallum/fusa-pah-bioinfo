Overview

Stage 6 uses the completed funannotate predict_v2 and annotate outputs to screen the predicted Fusarium solani proteome for genes and enzyme families potentially relevant to PAH transformation, xenobiotic metabolism, oxidative stress response, and environmental adaptation. The workflow proceeds in layers, from broad annotation-based screening through domain validation, Galaxy-based functional enrichment, targeted sequence similarity validation, and finally a ranked and integrated candidate table.

The isolate was recovered from a PAH-contaminated environment. This provides ecological context for the candidate search but does not itself constitute evidence of PAH degradation activity.

Search Strategy

Three complementary evidence layers are used across the Stage 6 workflow. Candidates detected by multiple layers are eligible for higher manual confidence ranking during 6.6. The 6.1 script records evidence strength but does not automatically assign final confidence.

LayerMethodSensitivitySpecificity
1Keyword search of annotation text fields (product name, note)HighLow
2Pfam domain accession matchingMediumHigh
3Homology via BLAST/DIAMOND against reference databasesMediumMedium

eggNOG and KEGG are not part of the core Pyrene pipeline due to confirmed database access blocks. They are added as optional enrichment layers via Galaxy in 6.4.

6.1 First-Pass Candidate Screen

The script parses every mRNA feature in the annotated GFF3 and searches three fields per gene: product= (product name free text), Dbxref=PFAM: (Pfam domain accessions), and note= (MEROPS, BUSCO, and other database hits). Multiple family matches per gene are preserved. The script does not automatically upgrade confidence. All confidence assessments remain at family-level priors pending manual review in 6.6. 

python scripts/stage6a_candidate_screen.py \
  --gff results/annotation/funannotate/predict_v2/annotate_results/Fusarium_solani.gff3 \
  --outdir results/stage6

6.2 Candidate Table Curation — Pending

The raw candidate table from 6.1 will be manually reviewed and curated to separate well-supported candidates from broad or noisy categories. CYP_general hits will be filtered to retain only those with explicit cytochrome P450 product annotation or PF00067 Pfam support. SDR, ALDH, ABC, and MFS hits with keyword-only evidence and no Pfam support will be downgraded. All contamination-flagged hits will be individually reviewed. 6.5 Targeted BLAST/DIAMOND Validation — Pending

Targeted BLAST or DIAMOND will be run after candidate proteins are extracted. Priority targets include CYP candidates, laccase and multicopper oxidase candidates, peroxidases, epoxide hydrolases, GSTs, aryl sulfatases, and all contamination-flagged hits. CYP candidates will require specialist classification using FCPD, curated CYP reference comparison, BLAST/DIAMOND, or phylogenetic analysis. FCPD classification and BLAST are complementary and should not be treated as equivalent.

Expected output: results/stage6/blast_validation.tsv

6.7 High-Priority Family Review — Pending

Primary catalytic candidates: CYPs, epoxide hydrolases, laccases and multicopper oxidases, peroxidases, FAD-dependent monooxygenases, aryl sulfatases, GSTs.

Secondary and support candidates: aldehyde dehydrogenases, formaldehyde dehydrogenases, alcohol oxidases, SDRs, ABC and MFS transporters, oxidative stress enzymes.

Contamination-sensitive: dioxygenases, catechol and protocatechuate and gentisate pathway enzymes, flagellar proteins, GroEL-like proteins, XoxF-like proteins, bacterial import and transport proteins.

6.8 Poorly Annotated Candidate Search — Pending

After recognisable candidate families are mapped, proteins annotated as hypothetical or with only partial domain support will be investigated. This step looks for proteins that are annotated as hypothetical protein with a relevant Pfam domain, vaguely annotated as hydrolase or oxidoreductase or monooxygenase or transferase without a specific product name, possibly secreted based on SignalP results, or located near candidate metabolism gene clusters on the same scaffold. The goal is not to claim novel genes. The goal is to identify uncharacterised proteins of possible interest for future investigation.

Expected output: results/stage6/uncharacterized_candidate_proteins.tsv

What Would Be Interesting to Find

The most scientifically meaningful result would be a coherent genomic toolkit rather than isolated hits: a combination of CYPs, epoxide hydrolases, GSTs, aryl sulfatases, aldehyde dehydrogenases, secreted laccases or peroxidases, and transporters. This would support the hypothesis that this strain carries a genomic repertoire consistent with fungal PAH transformation and detoxification.

The most interesting specific discovery would be CYPs assignable to PAH-relevant subfamilies (CYP5144, CYP504, CYP52, CYP53) via FCPD classification or phylogenetic analysis. PF00067 alone detects all CYPs equally and cannot distinguish subfamilies.

The most publishable direction would combine genome annotation with comparative genomics showing enrichment of xenobiotic metabolism enzyme families relative to reference Fusarium genomes, ideally supported by experimental degradation or expression data.
